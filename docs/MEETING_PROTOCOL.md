# Meeting Protocol

## Opening board

Every work session begins with five visible lines:

```text
Frozen target:
What counts as proof:
What counts as disproof:
Current central lemma:
Verification command or reviewer:
```

No new grand target is introduced until the session ends. Side ideas go to the parking lot with an owner and a resumption condition.

## Roles

- **Chair:** enforces target and time boxes; does not decide truth.
- **Scribe:** updates ledgers and records failed mechanisms verbatim.
- **Prover:** advances the selected route.
- **Breaker:** seeks counterexamples to each central lemma.
- **Lean/checker operator:** turns statements into executable interfaces.
- **Source auditor:** verifies historical claims and prevents citation drift.
- **Independent referee:** skips route-development meetings when possible.

One person may hold two roles, except that the prover of a central lemma should not be its only referee.

## Speaking rule for cross-disciplinary claims

A proposed step must be restated three ways:

1. formal-language statement with all quantifiers;
2. algebra/group statement identifying the recognition object;
3. Lean/checker acceptance condition.

A step is not accepted merely because one community recognizes the vocabulary.

## Checkpoints

At 25, 55, and 85 minutes:

```text
Strongest verified artifact:
Largest unverified assumption:
Smallest counterexample tested:
Remaining budget:
Continue / split / stop:
```

## End-of-session packet

Each group returns:

- exact claim attempted;
- files or equations produced;
- tests/reviews run;
- strongest result and sharpest failure;
- assumptions introduced;
- one next action that can finish in a single session.

The scribe records social/process failures separately from mathematical failures. Examples: unequal access to tools, uncredited integration work, pressure to announce, or a senior participant preventing falsification tests.
